Ceramic Minor

A research into developing a technique to provide me with complete freedom when creating ceramic shapes. The concept was born from a desire to enable myself to translate my sketches directly into objects. I experimented with how far I could push the boundaries of conventional ceramic mould making, developing a technique using sewn together fabric which was used as a mould. After development this process could successfully cast both plaster and clay and therefore enabled me to create almost any object I could draw. I am in continuation with this research currently and am trying to develop the moulds so that they are easily removable and reusable. This project demonstrates my approach to design and my interest in creating an innovative overall process.

A Future for Fish
  • Dezeen Article
  • Elle Decoration Article
  • Agri Meets Design Article

In collaboration with Anna Diljá Sigurðardóttir we explored the potential for vegetarian fish alternatives on the food market. Having noticed the abundance of meat alternatives we felt there was a severe lack of fish substitutes, despite the fish industry creating parallel environmental issues. Our project experimented with ways in which we could replicate the essence of the experience of eating a fish. We focussed primarily on paralleling the nutritional value and textures of fish. Providing an alternative to fish which was both aesthetically and nutritionaly very similar. This project was chosen to be part of the Embassy of Food during the Dutch Design Week 2017

SEX DINNER

Dutch Design Week 2016 Working within a group we created a three part sexual dining experience. The project focussed on creating dishes which when placed on the body, would create a multi-sensory but also sensual experience. The flavours incorporated aphrodisiacs which intensified as you worked up the arm. This event showed me the excitement of creating an event which a live audience experiences and was the beginning of me understanding the different factors involved when designing for a public audience.

HIGH FIDELITY RESEARCH
  • Click for Link to research publication

A research project undertaken with three other group members into the newly developed virtual world of High Fidelity. We designed alter egos and 3D scanned these to create avatars we could use in the virtual world. In this way we were able to meet other users and conduct our research. We were primarily focussed on understanding how public space is used differently in the virtual world and who is using it. During our research we were able to do in world interviews and be present during in world meetings between the CEO of high fidelity (Philip Rosedale) and other members of the team. As a result we presented our findings in a book and created a video depicting our transgression into this virtual world.

Aqua Terra

A project exploring aquaponics and hydroponics and how to bring these into the home. Using low tech solutions I designed an electricity free fridge, taking advantage of the natural cooling properties of sand and terra-cotta. The fridge would be supplied by its own aquaponic grow bed.
